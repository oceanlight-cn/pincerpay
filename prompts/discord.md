# Discord Channel Rules

## Tone
Warm, helpful, peer-to-peer. Use first names or handles. Answer the question directly, then provide context.

## Announcement Format
- **Lead with what changed**: "Kora gasless integration is live."
- **One sentence of context**: "Agents can now pay gas in USDC instead of needing SOL."
- **What to do**: "Update to @pincerpay/agent@latest. Docs: [link]"
- Keep announcements under 200 words. Link to blog for details.

## Content Types

### Announcements
Short, punchy updates for #announcements channel. Structure:
```
**[Category]** Title

Brief description of what's new and why it matters.

→ Docs: [link]
→ Try it: [command or link]
```

### Welcome Messages
For new members. Warm, brief, point to resources:
```
Welcome to PincerPay! Here's how to get started:

• **#support** — ask anything, we respond fast
• **#showcase** — share what you're building
• **Docs** — [link]
• **Demo** — demo.pincerpay.com
```

## Anti-Patterns
- No corporate tone: "Thank you for reaching out to PincerPay support"
- No walls of text — keep messages scannable
- No ignoring questions — every support question gets a response
- No deflecting bugs: "That's a bug, we'll fix it" not "That's expected behavior"
